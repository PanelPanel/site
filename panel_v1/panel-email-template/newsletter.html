Rothenberg & Jeffrey Kriksciun at Nationale, Portland, OR

Please join us for the inaugural gathering of PANEL L.A. featuring the performance work Skeleton, a light and sound piece by Sam Rowel and a dance by Rikki Rothenberg & Jeffrey Kriksciun

December 4, 2016, 8PM, Doors 7:30

SKELETON

Skeleton is an expanded cinema piece by the acoustic noise trio Sult and filmmaker Greg Pope.

Sult is comprised of members from Oslo and Oakland. (Jacob Felix Heule - percussion, Guro Skumsnes Moe - contrabass, Håvard Skaset - acoustic guitar.) Their music layers dense textures of acoustic sounds as vibrations run precariously aside one another - independent streams alternately blur into a unified mass.

Greg Pope founded film collective ‘Situation Cinema’ (Brighton 1986) and ‘Loophole Cinema’ (London, 1989). Working collaboratively and individually, Pope has made video installations, live art and single screen film works since 1996. These works include live cinema performance pieces ‘Light Trap’ and ‘Cipher Screen’ as well as 35mm film productions. His collaborations include working with sound artists Lee Paterson, John Hegre, Lasse Marhaug and Okkyung Lee.

SAM ROWELL

Meditative and mind-altering light and sound experience by Sam Rowell.

Sam Rowell is an artist and musician, she plays with multi-channel sonic environments that respond to continually shifting lighting arrays.

RIKKI ROTHENBERG & JEFFREY KRIKSCIUN

Rikki Rothenberg and Jeffrey Kriksciun will perform a dance.

Rikki Rothenberg creates dance work that is engaged with the unknown and the invisible. Rothenberg values the body’s innate knowledge of what is needed and holds this value at the core of all her work. Rikki is a psychotherapist in private practice and also makes visual art. For this untitled piece she has had the honor of being joined by Jeffrey Kriksciun.

Rooted in autodidacticism and interested in all mediums, Jeffrey Kriksciun's art work is multifaceted and experimental and inquisitive. Performing with Rikki has added a new dimension to his skateboarding and overall practice.
